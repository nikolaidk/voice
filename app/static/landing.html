An agentic workflow — humanised, and in action

From any document
to a produced voice & video story.

Point Fluent Agents Studio at a URL or a PDF. It writes the script, records the voice-over, designs the slides — figures and all — and hands you a podcast, a synced slideshow, a video, and a PowerPoint. This is not an app with AI features bolted on — it is a multi-agent pipeline dressed up for human interaction. You direct everything in plain English; you never touch an editing tool.

__HERO_NOTE__
An experiment — not a product

Everything on this site is the artifact of a deliberate under-specification experiment: an empty directory, one prompt — "create a web API that takes a URL or PDF and converts it into a podcast" — and fewer than 500 human words of direction in total, while Claude Code (Fable 5) made the engineering decisions. The author works professionally on complex agentic harness development across different environments — in this experiment, Claude Code itself was the harness. Roughly 3,400 lines of working code, 17 endpoints and this studio emerged in a single evening; the code review that redirected its architecture was four words long. What you are browsing demonstrates a way of working — the code is experimental, not production-ready: not security-hardened, not load-tested, not something to deploy as-is. What it does

Five specialised agents — extract, script, deck, voice, render — each with a defined job and hand-off, behind a workbench where every stage is directable and revisable in plain English. Agentic pain relief.

Modes

Podcast · Summary · Readout

A two-host conversation, a tight spoken digest, or the full document read aloud — tailored to the audience you describe in one sentence.

Slides

Decks that design themselves

Real images lifted from your source, and charts, timelines and diagrams the model draws itself as themed SVG. Optional animations throughout.

Voice

Directable narration

Personalities, hundreds of voices, and per-line direction: "line 4, slower, pause after the question — and pronounce Nguyen as win."

Iteration

Revise anything, in English

Script, slides, voice, timing, even the source document — each layer revisable in place, with the whole history kept on the project.

Identity

Your brand, extracted

Upload a PowerPoint template, a style guide, or a screenshot you like — colors and fonts are derived and applied to every output.

Outputs

Five files — and a publish button

MP3 audio, a timestamped transcript, a self-contained web slideshow, an MP4 video with optional captions, a PowerPoint with speaker notes — and one-click publishing to YouTube, private by default.
